Our Story

We're Benedict and Laurence — two brothers from San Antonio, Texas who fell in love with film photography. What started as a hobby turned into something bigger when Benedict began tearing apart broken Nikon cameras and figuring out how to bring them back to life.

Now we buy broken vintage cameras, repair them by hand, and sell them to people who want to shoot film without worrying about whether their gear actually works.

Benedict handles the repairs, sourcing, and photography. Laurence runs the business side — pricing, inventory, and making sure the numbers add up. We split everything 50/50 and do this because we genuinely love it.

Every camera we sell has been through our hands. We know what's inside it, what we fixed, and that it works. That's the whole point.

Our Repair Process

What happens between "broken" and "ready to shoot"

01

Source

We find broken Nikon bodies — FE, FM, F2 — from estate sales, online marketplaces, and local sellers.

02

Diagnose

Full inspection of the shutter, meter, film advance, light seals, and mirror mechanism.

03

Repair

Disassemble, clean, replace worn parts, re-lubricate, and reassemble. Every repair is done by hand.

04

Test

We run a roll of film through every camera. If the photos don't come out right, we go back to step two.
