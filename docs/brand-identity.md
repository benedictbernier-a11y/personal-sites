# Brand Identity Guide

## Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex |
|------|-------|-----|
| Primary | Darkroom Black | `#1A1A1A` |
| Secondary | Warm Amber | `#D4883A` |
| Accent | Burnt Sienna | `#A0522D` |
| Neutral | Cream | `#F5F0E8` |

Warm, analog, cohesive — tungsten light, leather straps, wooden workbenches.

## Typography

- **Headlines:** Condensed sans-serif — DIN Condensed, Barlow Condensed, or Tusker Grotesk. Bold and confident.
- **Body:** Clean geometric sans — Inter, General Sans, or Satoshi. Modern and readable.
- **Accent (optional):** Monospace — JetBrains Mono or Space Mono. For specs, model numbers, and technical details.

Avoid script or overly polished fonts. The brand should feel hands-on.

## Visual Style

- **Photography:** Warm natural light, shallow depth of field, shot on film when possible. Show hands, workbenches, the repair process. Grain and imperfection are on-brand.
- **Website:** Clean layout, cream whitespace, big product photos. "Independent magazine" feel over typical e-commerce.
- **Social media:** Before/after repairs, flat lays, sample photos from each camera, short repair process reels. Authentic over polished.

## Logo Concepts

1. **Wordmark + Aperture Mark** — Clean condensed type with an aperture icon integrated into a letter. Minimal, scales well.
2. **Badge / Seal** — Circular or rounded badge with name, "San Antonio, TX," "Est. 2026," and a simple camera/lens element. Sticker and stamp ready.
3. **Stacked Type + Icon** — Name in two lines with a standalone icon mark (lens, film canister, or shutter blade). Icon works alone as a social avatar.

**Recommendation:** Lead with the badge as your primary logo, use a simplified wordmark as your secondary/digital logo.
