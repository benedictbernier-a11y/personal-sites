# Financial Model — Film Camera Repair & Resale

## Assumptions

- Buy price per broken camera: $15-40
- Sell price per working camera: $50
- Shipping cost per camera (to buyer): $9
- Repair parts cost per camera: ~$5 (light seals, cleaning supplies)
- Shipping cost to us (when buying online): ~$5-10
- eBay fees (if listing there): ~13% of sale price ($6.50 per camera)
- Tools: already have / ordering separately (see Startup Costs)

## Cost Per Camera

| Expense | Low End | High End |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase price (broken) | $15 | $40 |
| Shipping to us | $5 | $10 |
| Repair parts | $3 | $5 |
| **Total cost to acquire & fix** | **$23** | **$55** |

## Revenue Per Camera

| Scenario | Revenue | Cost (mid) | Shipping to Buyer | eBay Fees (13%) | Profit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sell on our website** | $50 | $35 | $9 | $0 | **$6** |
| **Sell on eBay** | $50 | $35 | $9 | $6.50 | **-$0.50** |

> At $50 sell price with mid-range costs ($35), eBay barely breaks even. Selling on our own website or raising the price to $60+ makes eBay viable.

## Profit Split

- All costs split 50/50
- All revenue split 50/50
- Track per-camera: buy price + parts + shipping + fees = cost, sell price - cost = profit

## Key Assumptions

- Not every camera is fixable — budget for 1 in 5 being a dud (cost of duds is built into your average)
- Sell-through rate improves as reputation and listings grow
- eBay fees are ~13% (final value fee + payment processing)
- Margins improve significantly if we sell on our own website instead of eBay
- Repair costs are minimal since tools are a one-time purchase
- Shipping to buyer is ~$9 (USPS Priority Mail)

## Key Takeaway

At a $50 sell price, margins are tight — especially on eBay. The business gets much healthier at $60-75 per camera or by selling on your own website to avoid platform fees. Focus on models that command higher prices (Nikon F2) and keep buy prices low.
